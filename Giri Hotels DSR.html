Section 02  /  Business Case + Proposed Solution

The Problem You're Solving — and How Reeco Closes It.

Four pain points. Four direct answers.

01
The Problem

Manual Invoice Processing at Scale

Today, invoices arrive at each property by mail or email and get manually coded by property staff, then handed to an accounting team to enter into M3. Coding a single invoice by hand can take ten minutes or more — and at Giri that work happens twice, once at the property level and again at corporate. When invoices go missing or arrive late, vendor relationships suffer, credits get missed, and financial reporting is delayed.

The Answer

AI-Coded Invoices in Under One Minute

Reeco captures every invoice directly — no manual upload, no keying into M3. AI applies the correct GL code based on your M3 chart of accounts, learning from every invoice processed. Coding accuracy improves continuously. Approval workflows arrive as mobile push notifications, and every approved invoice syncs to M3 in real time as an unposted transaction — fully ready for your AP team to process the payment.

02
The Problem

No PO System Means No Spend Visibility Before the Invoice Arrives

Without a dedicated purchasing platform, spend isn't visible until invoices hit accounting. GMs have no real-time view of whether they're running over budget on a GL code, and there's no easy way to compare prices across suppliers — so it's hard to be sure each property is buying the right items, from the right supplier, at the right price. Corporate has no way to enforce purchasing compliance against GPO contracts or brand standards before an order is placed.

The Answer

Purchase Against Your M3 Budget — Before You Spend

Reeco pulls your M3 forecasts in real time so every purchase order is measured against actual budget before it's submitted. GMs see their GL exposure during checkout, and live price comparison across every supplier sits right in the cart — so your team can choose the best price and save money when it makes sense. Corporate configures approval thresholds and notifications. Curated order guides enforce HSM contract compliance and brand standards at the item level — across every property, every department.

03
The Problem

Inventory Counts Live on Paper and in Spreadsheets

Counts are done by hand on clipboards and spreadsheets, then keyed in later. The numbers are out of date the moment they're entered, on-hand value is hard to trust, and there's no reliable way to see variance or waste between counts. Audits are slow, and the pricing behind the count rarely reflects what you're actually paying today.

The Answer

Mobile Counts With Always-Current Pricing

Reeco's inventory module turns counts into a few taps on a phone — open the app, count down the list, and your report is ready instantly, with pricing that's always up to date. Audit setup is simple, and theoretical inventory from Toast POS sales data surfaces waste and variance automatically, so you can see exactly where product is going.

04
The Problem

F&B Food Cost and Recipe Management Lives in Spreadsheets

Recipe costing is maintained manually in Excel, disconnected from current supplier pricing. Food cost visibility depends on when someone last updated a spreadsheet — menu decisions lag reality, waste goes undetected, and margin erosion is discovered after the fact.

The Answer

Live Recipe Costs Tied to What You Actually Pay

Reeco's culinary module connects each recipe to live ingredient pricing from your supplier catalog. When supplier prices change, recipe costs update automatically. Pair it with Toast POS sales data and real-time inventory counts and you have food cost visibility by dish, by outlet, by period — without a single spreadsheet.

Section 07  /  FAQs

Questions We Expect — Answered Directly.

If something's missing, email Bill and we'll add it before the meeting.

"We're also evaluating Birch Street. Why choose Reeco?"
Several things come up in every Birch Street comparison. First, M3 charges a $550 setup fee and $910 per month subscription for their Birch Street integration — Reeco is the only procurement platform M3 has waived those fees for entirely. Second, Birch Street uses a punch-out model: your team selects a supplier, leaves the platform, and orders from that supplier's external website. Reeco is a consolidated marketplace — all your suppliers in one place, with live price comparison across every vendor simultaneously. Third, Birch Street does not have the M3 forecast and budget integration Reeco has — budgets must be manually uploaded. And fourth, Birch Street does not have a confirmed Toast POS integration. If inventory and culinary are on the roadmap, that integration matters.
"How hard is the implementation? We've been through difficult rollouts before."
Reeco does not require a dedicated full-time resource from your team to manage the rollout. AP automation alone goes live in about one week — roughly one hour of your team's time for setup. Adding Purchasing & Receiving extends that to three to four weeks, with the majority of the work handled by Reeco's onboarding team configuring suppliers and product data on the back end. A full implementation plan specific to Giri Hotels — property sequencing, M3 integration steps, and exact timeline — will be presented and discussed at the proposal meeting.
"Will the M3 integration actually work for our setup?"
Yes. M3 and Reeco have a direct, real-time API integration — the only procurement platform for which M3 has waived integration fees. Once a Web Services user is configured with the correct role permissions, your GL codes, suppliers, and forecasts pull into Reeco automatically. Invoices approved in Reeco sync back to M3 as unposted transactions, where your AP team reviews and posts them. There are over 30 management groups using both M3 and Reeco today. Bill can provide a direct reference from a group of comparable size and structure.
"What about adoption at the property level?"
Reeco is built for the user who is not a procurement professional. It looks and behaves like a consumer shopping experience — search for an item, see prices from all your suppliers side-by-side, submit a purchase order in a few taps. Approvals come through as push notifications on your phone — no logging into a system, no web portal. Receiving happens in the same mobile app. The learning curve is measured in hours, not days. Punch-out platforms like Birch Street require your team to navigate multiple supplier websites — adoption issues with that model are consistently cited by groups that have evaluated both.
"How is our data protected?"
Reeco is SOC 2 Type II certified. The full audit report is available upon request — email billg@reeco.com and we'll send it within 24 hours.
